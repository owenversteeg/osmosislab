Introduction

 This is Owen, Brendan, Erik, and James's Osmosis Lab. Here we'll explain every aspect of our 3 experiments, including our

 materials, methods, results, and observations. Finally, we'll discuss our results.

Questions Posed

"What will happen to yams soaked in unknown substances vs. a control?"

"What will the rate of diffusion be with various substances (Ovalbumin, Sucrose, Glucose, NaCl & dist. water) inside & outside dialysis tubing?"

"What will the rate of diffusion be through dialysis tubing with various percentages of sucrose inside the cell and water outside the cell?"

The answers to these questions are important because:

1) they enable us to identify the rate of diffusion for various substances

2) they enable us to identify the unknown substances, and

3) they let us find the rate of diffusion of various concentrations of sucrose through dialysis tubing.

Some Scientific Background

Diffusion is the movement of molecules from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration.

Thus, a higher rate of diffusion = more molecules being moved from one area to another.

Ovalbumin is a protein. The control in all of our experiments is a soln. of 100% distilled water.

Osmosis is the movement of a solvent (as water) through a semipermeable membrane (as of a living cell) into a solution of higher solute concentration that tends to equalize the concentrations of solute on the two sides of the membrane.

Hypotheses

1) We hypothesized that the yams will gain some mass, but the amount gained will vary.

2) We hypothesized that as the % of the cell's soln. that is sucrose increases, the rate of diffusion will decrease.

3) We hypothesized that the more ovalbumin on one side of a cell's wall, the more the other side will gain mass
